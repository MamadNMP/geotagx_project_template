Can you see small pools of water?

Small pools of water can include puddles, ponds, irrigation channels, ditches, and water accumulating in garbage

How do I answer this question? Get some help

During and after the monsoon season, people living along the Yamuna suffer severely from mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever. Mosquitos lay their eggs in stagnant (non-moving) water bodies. Heavy rains and receding flood waters leave behind puddles of stagnant water, which, create perfect breeding grounds for mosquitos . With this question we are trying to find areas that are at a high risk of dengue fever due to the presence of these stagnant water bodies.

You should be looking for any water that is not moving or not regularly flushed out. This includes small irrigation channels, puddles, ponds, and water accumulating in open drains (nullahs), ditches or garbage like old tyres, plastics or soft drink cans.

Can you see any signs of fast moving water? Check all that apply

How do I answer this question? Get some help

Fast moving flood waters are dangerous. They can wash away people and property and make crossing the river near impossible.

Fast moving water can be identified by the following indicators:

  • White peaks in the water and rough surface – fast moving water can form waves on the surface, which are indicated by the white peaks and rough, choppy water surface.
  • Water swirling around objects – As fast moving water hits objects in its path such as buildings, trees, poles, and vehicles, it will rise up around them.

Can you see any of the following flood protection measures? Check all that apply

How do I answer this question? Get some help

In this question you are looking for signs that protective measures have been put up against the floods. This can include temporary measures like sandbags (what are sandbags?), or makeshift barriers, and permanent measures like embankments. An embankment is a man-made hill running along the river bank that raises the level of the river bank and so stops water from flooding surrounding areas when it rises. An embankment could be made from a variety of materials including concrete and soil (what is an embankment?). See the photos below for examples of different flood protection measures.
